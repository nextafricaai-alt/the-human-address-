The Weight of Being Made

Delivered June 14, 2026 · 18 minutes
The Address in Writing

My friends — I want to begin not with what we can build, but with what we are. We have become a people fluent in capability and illiterate in meaning. We can extend a life and cannot say what a life is for.

To be made is to be answerable. This is the sentence I want to leave with you tonight. A thing that makes itself owes nothing to anyone. But you and I did not make ourselves. We were intended — thought of before we arrived, named before we could answer. And to be intended is to carry a weight that no achievement can add to and no failure can remove.

We can extend a life and cannot say what a life is for.

In my own continent, the one the world insists on calling young, we have always known something that the optimised world is only beginning to grieve: that a person is not a resource, that a family is the first government, that the dead are not gone and the unborn are not absent. We have known that to be human is to stand in a line of obligation that runs both ways through time.

So I will not ask you tonight to do more. I will ask you to remember more. To remember that you were made, and that the One who made you meant something by it. The recovery of our age will not begin with a better tool. It will begin with a better answer to the oldest question — and the willingness to live as though the answer were true.

Until next week — sit well, and be reminded of what you are.
— Hudson
